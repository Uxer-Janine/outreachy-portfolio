Overview

Personal & Community Background

I am a Data Scientist with a background in Product Design, bringing together analytical thinking, problem-solving, and a strong foundation in user-centered design.

I come from the Kuria community in Kenya, a traditionally marginalized community where opportunities for girls are often limited. Being the firstborn in a family of six, I have had to navigate and overcome a range of social and economic challenges to pursue education and a career in technology.

I was fortunate to have a father who believed in me despite societal expectations. He invested in my education, supported my learning, and played an active role in shaping how I think, study, and approach challenges. He remains a significant part of my journey.

My mother has been a constant source of support, often offering perspective and grounding during difficult moments. Even without a technical background, her advice has helped me stay balanced and approach problems with clarity.

I am also supported by my siblings, many of whom are in tech, and a close friend who has been deeply involved in reviewing my work and supporting my Outreachy journey. This strong support system has played a meaningful role in my growth.

Professional Background

My journey into tech began in design, where I learned how to approach complex problems creatively and keep users at the center of every decision. I now bring that same mindset into data science—asking the right questions, uncovering patterns, and translating insights into meaningful impact.

I recently completed an intensive Data Science bootcamp, where I have built hands-on experience in Python, SQL, Pandas, Scikit-learn, and core machine learning techniques including regression, classification, and clustering. My work spans exploratory data analysis, predictive modeling, and data visualization across real-world datasets.

Outreachy Contributions

As part of my Outreachy application, I contributed to the Fedora ecosystem through projects involving Docling and Ramalama. My work focused on building and analyzing OCR pipelines for multilingual documents, comparing outputs across different approaches, and exploring how structured data can be used for downstream AI applications.

Beyond technical contributions, I actively engaged in the community—initiating and coordinating peer reviews, structuring collaborative documentation, and participating in open discussions with contributors and mentors.

This portfolio brings together my technical work, writing, collaboration, and reflections throughout the contribution phase.

It showcases not just what I built, but how I think, collaborate, and grow within open-source environments.

Key Contributions

1. Document Processing & OCR Pipeline (Docling)

Built and analyzed a multilingual document processing pipeline using Docling and Tesseract. Processed Swahili and French documents, converted them into structured Markdown, and evaluated output quality across different document types.

Impact: Demonstrated how OCR output quality varies based on document structure and highlighted the importance of validation before downstream AI use.

2. Peer Review System & Collaboration (HackMD)

Initiated and coordinated a structured peer review system for Outreachy contributors using HackMD. Designed the workflow, organized participants across multiple tasks, and created a centralized system for tracking reviews and contributions.

Impact: Improved collaboration, increased visibility of contributions, and created a scalable system for structured feedback across the team.

3. Data Analysis & Visualization

Analyzed OCR outputs using statistical methods, including word count comparison, line length distribution, and noise detection. Used visualizations to better understand structural differences between documents and OCR performance.

Impact: Provided deeper insight into OCR behavior beyond raw output, reinforcing the importance of analysis in document processing pipelines.

4. Technical Writing & Documentation

Authored a practical guide on navigating the Outreachy contribution phase, focusing on clear, actionable steps for new applicants.

Impact: Demonstrated ability to translate complex processes into clear, structured guidance for others.
